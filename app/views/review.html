Considerations to add depending on results:

  • Urban dense areas If metrics indicate rising air pollution, integrate detailed air quality monitoring and environmental health studies.
  • Rural and dispersed areas If local economies rely on agricultural and/or tourism activities, include targeted economic viability and diversification studies.
  • Mixed-user or urban fringe areas Map overlapping zones and potential conflicts to ensure cohesive policies between residential, commercial, and industrial uses.
  • Areas with notable heritage/conversation designations Coordinate with economic assessments to identify potential grants or incentives for conservation projects.
  • Rapidly growing/expanding areas Cross-reference with urban dense or mixed-use studies if growth is concentrated in particular districts.
  • Coastal areas Link coastal studies with sustainability assessments from urban or rural branches as needed, ensuring environmental recommendations dovetail with local infrastructure modifications.
  • Proximty to an airport Integrate with urban dense or growth studies if the area experiences overlapping urban pressures, aligning transport and housing deliverables with airport-related impacts.
  • Heavy tourism based areas Combine with cultural, environmental, and land-use studies to ensure tourism benefits are balanced against local resident needs and conservation priorities.
