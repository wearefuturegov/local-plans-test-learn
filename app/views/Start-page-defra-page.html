Gathering evidence in the 30-month plan preparation process

To produce a local plan within the 30-month plan preparation process, the evidence supporting the plan needs to be:

  • proportionate– you should produce all the evidence needed for examination to prevent delays
  • relevant– you do not need to produce a study if it is not necessary for your local plan
  • up-to-date– you do not need to redo evidence if it already exists, but existing evidence must be relevant

You should use your results as a guide, especially if you're still early in your plan-making. You should confirm your final list of evidence with the Planning Inspectorate at Gateway 2.
